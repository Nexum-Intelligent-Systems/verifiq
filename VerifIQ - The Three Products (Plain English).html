The problem we are solving

Construction documents have a hidden mess inside them.

Tender packs are the big bundle of paperwork that builders use to figure out what to build and how much it will cost. They are supposed to be perfect. They are not.

A real example from May 2026

One tender pack. One day of checking. 327 problems found.

We took a real Irish tender pack — drawings, specs, the whole bundle — and ran it through our checker. In one day we found:

A "Windows Schedule" that actually contained drawings of stairs.
A spec describing a school in County Mayo — except the building is in Dublin.
A mechanical spec written for a building with a boiler — except the project uses heat pumps.
VAT shown as 21% when the correct rate has been 23% since 2024.
Fire dampers spec'd to a British standard that was withdrawn in the 1970s.
327
problems found in one pack.
A senior expert would take 5 to 10 days to find this much.

Same engine. Different story.

Imagine a smoke detector.

Same product. Three stories. Three sales motions. Three buyers willing to pay different prices for different reasons.
VerifIQ is the same. The engine reads the same documents, finds the same things, but tells three different stories to three different buyers.

Why this works.

Three reasons, in plain English.

1

Same engine, three products

We do not need to build three separate things. The engine is the same. We just change how the output is framed for each buyer. Engineering cost: tiny. Revenue uplift: 2-3×.

2

Each buyer has different money

A designer pays for time-saving and reputation. A contractor pays for direct money saved on a bid. Same finding, but the contractor pays more because the ROI is bigger.

3

The Studio split opens a brand new market

Sole-practitioner architects could never justify €400 per check. They can justify €75 for an arch-only check. That is 1,500+ new firms we could not reach before.
