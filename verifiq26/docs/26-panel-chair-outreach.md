# VerifIQ — Panel Chair Outreach Pack

**Doc ID:** `verifiq-panel-outreach-v0.1`  
**Purpose:** Templates for approaching the first chartered reviewer panel chair. Per the Implementation Review Council (`docs/25`) and the project plan's Week 1 hard gate.  
**Voice:** Sober. No emojis. No startup-speak. Sentence case.  
**Date:** 2026-06-01

---

## Target profile — what good looks like

You're looking for **one** named chair to start. Profile:

- Chartered Irish design professional (RIAI architect, EI CEng, SCSI surveyor, or registered Assigned Certifier)
- 15+ years in capital delivery — public sector preferred
- Known in your network or one warm intro away
- Respected for technical judgement, not for being loud
- Time available for 4–6 hours per week from Week 4 onward (paid + equity)
- Comfortable saying "the AI got this wrong" in writing
- Not actively competing with VerifIQ (no rival service)
- Not so senior that a per-pack honorarium is meaningless to them

Avoid:

- People who'd want to take it over (founder-mode mismatch)
- People who've been burned by startups (will want bulletproof contracts you can't yet draft)
- People who've never read an AI output critically (won't catch bad findings)
- People in active commercial litigation involving design responsibility

— Liam
Founder, VerifIQ (a GovIQ product)
liam@goviq.ie · verifiq.ie

**Notes for sending:**

- Replace `{{mutual_contact}}` with the named intro source. If no warm intro exists, use Variant 2 instead — do not pretend you have one.
- Send Tuesday or Wednesday morning. Friday is dead air; Monday is overloaded.
- If they don't reply in 7 days, one single follow-up (Variant 3 below). No more.

## Compensation framing — what to say when asked

Have this answer ready. Don't improvise.

**The structure:**

> "Per-pack honorarium plus a small equity position. Honorarium is paid against each pack you review — so during pilot when there are 2-3 packs a month, that's modest. As volume grows, the honorarium scales with it. The equity position is a small percentage in VerifIQ as a product line of GovIQ Ltd, vesting over a couple of years. I'm working through the exact numbers with our solicitor; happy to share the term sheet once it's drafted. Comfortable in principle?"

**Don't quote a number until your solicitor has helped you structure it.** Per `docs/15-shoestring-bootstrap-ireland.md` and `docs/16-solo-reviewer-phase.md` — honorarium target is €150–€350 per reviewed pack (Option C in Doc 16), equity participation is 0.5–3% of VerifIQ line revenue or equivalent GovIQ equity.

**If they push for specifics:** "Honorarium expected in the €150–€350 per pack range. Equity is a structural conversation I want my solicitor to lead. I don't want either of us to commit to numbers before the engagement letter is drafted."

## What to send them ahead of the first conversation

After they accept the conversation request, send a single short email at least 24 hours before:

```
Subject: Ahead of our chat — three short reads

{{first_name}},

Thanks again. Three short reads if you have 10 minutes before we talk — none of them are required but they'll save us going over basics:

1. The product page: verifiq.ie (2 mins to scan)
2. The case study (the actual reviewed pack, anonymised): verifiq.ie/case-study-01.html (5 mins)
3. The reviewer scope I'm honest about: verifiq.ie/solo-reviewer-policy.html (3 mins — this is the bit I most want your read on)

See you {{day at time}}.

— Liam
```

The third link is the most important. The Solo Reviewer Phase policy tells a serious chartered professional exactly how honest you intend to be about what the panel does and doesn't sign. If they're going to chair, they need to know that posture is locked.

## The first 30 minutes — running the conversation

**Structure (30 minutes):**

- 5 min: their background, current commitments
- 10 min: show them the case study + one finding in depth — let them critique
- 5 min: explain the panel role + cadence + scope
- 5 min: compensation conversation (per script above)
- 5 min: what happens next

**Things you must say:**

- "I want a chair who'll find what's wrong with this, not a chair who'll defend it."
- "Findings outside your discipline aren't yours to sign — we mark those AI-surfaced·pending until the relevant specialist joins."
- "The engagement letter is drafted by our solicitor and you read it before anything is signed."
- "You can step back at any time — the equity vests on a schedule and unvested equity returns."
- "I'm not asking you to commit today. I'm asking for a yes to a follow-up conversation in 7–14 days once you've had time to look at the artefacts."

**Things you must not say:**

- "We're going to be huge."
- "It's basically already working."
- "Other reviewers have already said yes."  (Unless true.)
- "AI will do most of the work."  (Defeats the framing.)
- "The legal stuff is sorted."  (Until it actually is.)
