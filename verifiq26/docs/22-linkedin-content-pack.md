## 1 · Launch Post (publish on day 1)

```
After 12 years in Irish public-sector capital delivery, the same five
things kept derailing tender packs in the last week before issue:

1. Date for Substantial Completion left blank — LDs unenforceable.
2. BCAR document drafted for the wrong contract form.
3. Hoist (or door, or rooflight) named differently across spec, RDS,
   and schedule.
4. "I.S. EN 10101" cited — a standard prefix that doesn't exist.
5. Cause-and-effect matrix referenced but not appended.

Every one of these is recoverable in pre-tender. Every one becomes a
six-figure variation post-award.

So I built VerifIQ — a software-based reading aid for tender packs.
It reads against the live Irish corpus (I.S. / TGD / BCAR / CWMF /
PW-CF) and surfaces, in the documents' own words, what the registered
designer may wish to read closely.

It doesn't certify. It doesn't sign. It augments — it doesn't replace.
The chartered eye stays where it always was: with the designer.

Pilot cohort opens this week. Ten Irish practices, one pack each, at
early-pilot rates. 30% off the first ten.

verifiq.ie

— Liam
Founder, VerifIQ (a GovIQ Ltd product)
```

## 5 · Anti-patterns to avoid on LinkedIn

- **No "🔥 We're so excited to announce 🚀" posts.** Chartered audience reads tone as a signal of seriousness.
- **No motivational quotes.** None.
- **No "5 lessons I learned from a failed pitch" hot-take posts.** Reserved for people whose audience expects them.
- **No engagement bait** ("agree or disagree?" "what do you think?"). Substantive content earns substantive engagement.
- **No tagging executives** in launch posts. Looks desperate.
- **No "story-time" first-person dramatic openings.** "Yesterday I sat in a meeting and realised…" — these read as performance to a chartered audience.
- **No emojis.** Period.
- **No "growth hacking" tactics** — pods, comment-rings, bot follows. The chartered audience is small and notices.

## 6 · What to do with replies

| Reply type | Action |
|---|---|
| Genuinely interested → demo | Book within 48 hours. Use sales playbook (Doc 19) demo script. |
| Genuinely interested → "let me think" | Send case study link by email. No follow-up for 14 days. One single follow-up if no response. |
| Polite "not for us right now" | Thank, ask if they know someone who might find it useful, move on. Re-engage in 6 months. |
| Polite "not for us ever" | Thank. Don't argue. Re-engage in 12 months only if circumstances change. |
| Hostile or sceptical | Engage seriously. Address the substantive point. Don't get defensive. Most sceptics become loudest advocates if you respect their objection. |
| Sales pitch back at you | Decline politely; not the time. |
| Procurement / vendor-management referral | Take it seriously. Some practices have formal vendor onboarding that's actually fine for us. |
