# GTM-02 · LinkedIn content kit — First Read launch

**Status:** Ready to post · **Date:** 2026-06 · **Owner:** Liam (named reviewer)
**Companion to:** `docs/40-gtm-first-read-strategy.md`

Founder-led, case-study-fuelled. Every asset ends soft on the same ask:
*First Read, €29, your pack.* Drip over ~2 weeks. Voice is a chartered
reviewer who has lost weekends to this problem — specific, plain, never hypey.
No banned verbs (file 08): VerifIQ **surfaces / reads / flags**, it does not
"certify", "guarantee", "approve", or "ensure".

> Output is indicative. The reviewer verifies locally. Keep this posture in
> every post — it's the brand *and* the legal position.

## 0. The ICP — who to put this in front of

Target by title + practice size, in Ireland first.

- **Architects** — RIAI members; technical directors, associates, job
  architects at 10–200-person practices.
- **Engineers** — Engineers Ireland; M&E and C&S consultants, senior/principal
  engineers, technical leads.
- **QS / cost** — SCSI members; PQS and cost managers carrying tender packs.
- **Employer-side / capital teams** (later) — HSE Estates, OGP, local-authority
  capital projects, Section 38/39 bodies running PW-CF procurements.

Targeting on LinkedIn: filter connections + outreach by these bodies (RIAI,
Engineers Ireland, SCSI), by job title, and by Dublin/Cork/Galway practices.
Engage *before* you pitch — comment on their posts for a fortnight first.

## 1. Carousel — "327 findings on one real pack. Here are 5 that cost money."

PDF carousel, 1080×1350 portrait, bone/paper background, Playfair headings,
JetBrains Mono for the source quotes (mirror the website's source-quote block:
the quote, then a thin rule, then the document reference). One finding per
slide; every slide shows the *exact line in the exact document*.

**Slide 1 — Cover**
> **We read a real Stage 2C tender pack.**
> 161 documents. 327 findings. Source-quoted.
> Here are 5 that would have become variations.
> *Swipe →*
Footer: `VerifIQ · the pre-build read · indicative, you verify locally`

**Slide 2 — The blank that voids the damages**
- Finding: **Date for Substantial Completion — left blank.**
- Quote: `"Date for Substantial Completion: ____________"`
- Ref: *Form of Tender · Schedule Part 1 · §4.2 · p.12*
- Why it costs: a required entry left blank — liquidated damages are
  unenforceable as drawn. Caught pre-issue, it's a one-line fix; caught at
  award, it's a negotiation you've already lost.

**Slide 3 — The fire rating that doesn't match its own condition**
- Finding: **REI 30 specified against a planning condition that reads REI 60.**
- Quote: `"…fire resistance REI 30 to the separating construction…"`
- Ref: *Fire Safety spec · vs · Grant of Permission, Condition 3 (REI 60)*
- Why it costs: a coordination gap between the fire strategy and the conditions
  it's built on. Surfaced as an RFI now, or as a re-detailing instruction on site.

**Slide 4 — The standard that was withdrawn**
- Finding: **BS 476-7 cited — withdrawn, superseded by the EN 13501 suite.**
- Quote: `"…surface spread of flame to BS 476-7…"`
- Ref: *Architectural specification · Finishes · Clause 12.4*
- Why it costs: a withdrawn standard in a live spec is a soft target on review,
  and a genuine compliance gap against current Irish practice.

**Slide 5 — The wrong jurisdiction on the cover**
- Finding: **County named on the cover sheet doesn't match the site address.**
- Quote: `"…Co. [X]…"` against the address block on the same sheet.
- Ref: *Drawing cover sheet · title block vs address block*
- Why it costs: small, embarrassing, and exactly the kind of thing a whole-pack
  read catches that a discipline-by-discipline read never does.

**Slide 6 — The VAT line that's out of date**
- Finding: **VAT shown at 21% — the rate that applied before the change.**
- Quote: `"…inclusive of VAT at 21%…"`
- Ref: *Pricing document · Preliminaries*
- Why it costs: a stale figure that flows into the tender sum and the claim.

**Slide 7 — CTA**
> That's 5 of 327 — from **one** pack.
> The more you upload, the more it finds.
> **First Read — your first 25 drawings, €29.**
> Work email. One per practice. Register back in 24–48h.
> *Link in comments.* (Never put the link in the post body — it suppresses reach.)

## 2. The 6-second live-review video (the scroll-stopper)

Screen-record the homepage `#hero-review` animation (the Form-of-Tender
fragment) at the top of `index.html` / `first-read.html`. It already does the
whole story: scan sweep → blank found → leader line drawn → "Critical · C-03"
tag stamps in → the finding line types out.

**Storyboard (6s, silent-first, captioned):**
| t | On screen | Caption (burned-in) |
|---|-----------|---------------------|
| 0.0s | The clause fragment sits still | `A real tender clause.` |
| 0.5–2.0s | Scan sweep crosses the fragment | `VerifIQ reads it.` |
| 2.0–3.0s | Blank highlights, leader line draws to the tag | `Finds the blank.` |
| 3.0–4.0s | "Critical · C-03" stamps in | `Flags it. Critical.` |
| 4.0–6.0s | Finding line types out under the fragment | `Date for Substantial Completion — left blank.` |
| hold | End card | `First Read · €29 · your pack` |

**How to record:** open the page in a desktop browser, narrow to ~520px so the
fragment fills frame, hit **↻ Replay the read**, screen-record that region.
Export 1080×1080 (feed) and 1080×1920 (Stories/Reels). Add the burned-in
captions in any editor — most of LinkedIn watches muted.

**Post copy to pair with it:**
> This is six seconds of a junior-level first read on a real tender clause.
> The blank is a missing Date for Substantial Completion — it voids the
> liquidated-damages clause. We caught it in the drawing, not the variation.
> First Read on your pack: €29, link in comments.

## 4. Cadence & mechanics

- **Week 1:** Post A (Mon) → Video (Wed) → Carousel (Fri).
- **Week 2:** Post C build-in-public (Mon) → a single anonymised finding as a
  standalone "find of the week" (Wed) → Post B posture piece (Fri).
- **Always:** link in the first comment, not the body. Reply within the hour.
  Pin the carousel to your profile for the fortnight.
- **DM play:** when someone engages twice, soft DM — "want me to run 25 of your
  drawings? €29, register back in two days." No pitch deck.
- **Measure** against the GTM KPIs (`docs/40`, §7): First Reads sold, work-email
  conversion, and *which* finding earned the upsell — that line becomes next
  week's post.

> The case study is the campaign. Every delivered First Read is the next one.
