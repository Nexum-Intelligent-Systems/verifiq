# GovIQ — Three 3-Minute Pitch Scripts

**Version:** v0.4 | **Date:** 2026-06-16 | **Owner:** Liam McDonagh
**Use:** All three run against the v11 deck (same 10 slides). They differ only in the **opening hook and emphasis**. Pick the one that fits the room — or mix the best opening with your preferred close. Each is ~3 minutes at a measured pace (≈380–430 words).

**Platform beats now in every variant:** Procurement Router → **Contracts** (post-award) → **Budget Router** (Vote-to-spend traceability) → the **€5k–€50k invisible layer** (below-threshold purchases that complete with no file left).

---

## Buyer quote — "Are you replacing us?" (use in Type A or hybrid)

Real exchange from an estates manager in a pilot conversation. Use as a **human anchor** — it answers the fear every buyer has (automation = headcount) before you sell features.

**On slide (pull quote, Slide 2 or 4):**

| | |
|---|---|
| **Estates manager** | "Are you trying to replace us as project managers?" |
| **Liam** | "No. I'm trying to get us managing the projects — and doing less paperwork." |

**Spoken (Type A — say it like you remember the room):**

> An estates manager stopped me mid-demo and asked: "Are you trying to replace us as project managers?" I said no — I'm trying to get us back to *managing* the projects, and off the paperwork. That's the whole point.

**Why it works:** Reframes GovIQ from threat → enabler. The officer stays in charge; the system carries compliance, documents and audit trail so they can do the job they were hired for.

**Delivery tip:** Brief pause after their question. Deliver your answer flat and direct — no sales tone. Then move straight into what GovIQ actually does.

Real feedback after a demo with a senior HSE colleague. Use as **social proof** — insider validation that this isn't a vendor pitch, it's built by someone who knows the system from the inside.

**On slide (Slide 7 Traction or Slide 8 Close — brass pull quote):**

> "Never in forty years have I seen something this innovative come from someone working in the HSE. Well done."

— *Senior HSE colleague* *(confirm name/title before putting on slide)*

**Spoken (Type A — just before the pilot line):**

> After a recent demo, a senior HSE colleague said to me: "Never in forty years have I seen something this innovative come from someone working in the HSE. Well done." That's why we're not pitching a concept.

**Spoken (Type B — lighter touch, one line before close):**

> A senior HSE colleague put it plainly after a demo: never in forty years had they seen anything this innovative from inside the system. The pilot goes live in August.

**Why it works:** Pairs with the estates-manager quote — first quote answers *fear* (replacement); this one answers *skepticism* (another tool from outside). Forty years = institutional gravity. "From someone working in the HSE" = you're one of them, not a vendor.

**Delivery tip:** Don't oversell it. Quote them, pause, move on. If asked who said it, have name and role ready — or use "senior HSE estates colleague" if that's what you're cleared to say.

**Compliance note:** Spoken pitch can use "a senior HSE colleague." On-slide or in investor materials, confirm they're happy to be quoted (or keep anonymous: "Senior HSE colleague, 40+ years in public capital").

## Type A — "The Insider" (founder + human story first)

**Angle:** Lead with your 20 years and the day-to-day reality. Warm, credible, story-led.
**Best for:** A room that buys people first; when you want them to like and trust you fast.
**Opens on:** Slide 1 → 8 (you) → 2/3/4 (problem) → 5–7 (product) → 9/10 (pilot, ask).

> Hi, I'm Liam McDonagh. I've spent twenty years inside Irish public procurement — writing tenders, running evaluations, delivering capital works — so let me tell you how it actually goes.
>
> A public body needs to buy something. The procurement officer opens the last similar tender, copies it, updates a spreadsheet, and chases approvals over email. It takes fourteen to twenty weeks. And one copied mistake — a wrong route, an outdated clause, a missing justification — can get the whole thing challenged and re-run from scratch. Forty percent of reviewed tenders carry a defect like that.
>
> It's not that people are careless. It's that there's no controlled system — the rules live in three or four people's heads. And when it goes wrong, it doesn't stay local. Tusla, Beaumont, the HSE have all been called before the Public Accounts Committee to explain procurement they couldn't defend — hundreds of millions, flagged by auditors.
>
> An estates manager stopped me mid-demo and asked: "Are you trying to replace us as project managers?" I said no — I'm trying to get us back to managing the projects, and off the paperwork. That's the whole point.
>
> That's the pain I built GovIQ to remove. GovIQ routes each procurement to the correct procedure, generates the documents, tracks approvals, and captures every decision as a court-defensible audit trail. It's not a black box, and it's not AI deciding for you — it's the real rules, applied consistently, with the evidence built from day one. It roughly halves the timeline, without touching the statutory windows the law requires.
>
> But routing the tender is only half the story. After award, **Contracts** takes over — variations, payments, practical completion — on the same file, not a fresh spreadsheet. **Budget Router** traces every euro from Vote allocation down through departments and funded providers — Section 38, Section 39, NGOs — and flags the wrong procurement route *before* money is committed. And here's the layer nobody tracks: thousands of five-to-fifty-thousand-euro purchases every year — quotes, mini-competitions, routine call-offs — that get completed and then simply vanish. No file. No trail. When the auditor asks, it's gone. GovIQ captures those too.
>
> What makes it hard to copy is that it's twenty years of my judgement turned into software — a new entrant can't replicate that. After a recent demo, a senior HSE colleague said: "Never in forty years have I seen something this innovative come from someone working in the HSE. Well done." And it's real: our first pilot, a Section 39 organisation, goes live in August; the platform is built and tested; and we're in active conversations across the public sector, including the HSE.
>
> GovIQ gives public bodies the evidence before the question arrives. We're not pitching a concept — we're closing pilots on a product built for a real, and very costly, system failure. Thank you.

**Delivery tip:** Slow down on "twenty years" and "fourteen to twenty weeks." Let the Tusla/Beaumont/HSE line land, then the estates-manager exchange — pause after their question, answer flat, no sales tone. Pause after "simply vanish" — that's your third hook.

## Type B — "The Governance Shield" (accountability cold-open)

**Angle:** Open cold on the scandal. Hard-hitting, urgent, sharpest wedge. Reframes GovIQ from "workflow tool" to "governance shield."
**Best for:** A tired or mid-pack slot where you need to grab the room in 10 seconds; audiences who know the public sector.
**Opens on:** Slide 4 (accountability) → 2/3 (why it happens) → 5–7 (the shield) → 8/9/10 (you, pilot, ask).

> Tusla. Beaumont Hospital. The HSE. All three were called before the Public Accounts Committee to explain procurement they couldn't defend — the HSE alone, three hundred and eighty-two million euro outside the rules since 2020.
>
> This is one of the most common governance failures in the Irish public sector, and almost nobody is fixing it at the source. I'm Liam McDonagh, founder of GovIQ.
>
> Here's why it happens. A tender takes fourteen to twenty weeks, and teams still build them from copied templates, spreadsheets and email. One wrong route, one missing justification, and the file can't stand up — forty percent of reviewed tenders carry a defect. The officer doesn't find out for months, until the auditor, the C&AG, or the Committee comes asking one question: can you prove it? By then it's too late to build the evidence.
>
> And the big tenders are only part of it. Every year, thousands of purchases between five and fifty thousand euro — quotes, mini-competitions, small call-offs across departments and funded providers — get completed and then disappear. No central file. No trace. That's where compliance actually breaks.
>
> GovIQ is the shield — end to end. It routes each procurement to the correct procedure, generates the documents, tracks every approval, and builds a court-defensible audit trail from the first click — so the answer exists before the question. After award, **Contracts** carries variations, payments and completion on the same file, not a fresh folder of email. **Budget Router** ties spend back to the Vote, through every department and Section 39 or NGO line, and stops the wrong route before a euro moves — so funded providers can't spend outside the rules and the parent body can see it in real time. It's rules-based, not a black box, built on the real frameworks.
>
> What makes it defensible is me — twenty years inside this system, the judgement encoded in the product. A senior HSE colleague said after a demo: never in forty years had they seen anything this innovative from inside the system. And it's live: a Section 39 pilot goes live in August, the platform is built and tested, and the HSE is in the pipeline.
>
> GovIQ turns procurement from an audit liability into an audit asset. We give public bodies the evidence before the question arrives. Thank you.

**Delivery tip:** Say the three names slowly, like a roll call, then pause. That silence is the hook. The "five and fifty thousand" line is your second pause — let the room feel the invisible spend.

## Type C — "The Operator" (product + traction first, investor style)

**Angle:** Lead with what's built and what's moving. Confident, numbers-forward, momentum-led.
**Best for:** Investor-heavy rooms that reward traction and clarity over story.
**Opens on:** Slide 1 → 6/5 (product) → 2/4 (problem + stakes) → 8 (moat) → 9/10 (traction, ask).

> I'm Liam McDonagh, founder of GovIQ. The headline: we have a working product, a pilot going live in August, and a market worth over twenty billion euro a year to aim at.
>
> GovIQ is the procurement control layer for the Irish public sector — not a point tool, a full stack. The router, document generator, evaluation and audit modules are built and tested today. A procurement officer uploads a requirement; GovIQ routes it, generates the pack, tracks approvals, and signs the audit trail. After award, **Contracts** takes over: variations, payments, practical completion. **Budget Router** traces allocation from central Vote to department and funded-provider spend — Section 38, Section 39, NGOs — with procurement obligation checks before a euro is committed. One platform, one audit chain, from intent to invoice.
>
> That matters because public procurement is slow and hard to defend: fourteen to twenty weeks per major tender, forty percent carrying a procedural defect. And when it goes wrong it lands at the Public Accounts Committee — Tusla, Beaumont, the HSE, hundreds of millions flagged by auditors. But the bigger volume is invisible: thousands of five-to-fifty-thousand-euro purchases every year — quotes, call-offs, mini-competitions across departments and funded providers — that complete untracked and leave no file when the auditor arrives six months later. GovIQ captures that layer too, and halves major-tender timelines without touching the statutory windows the law requires.
>
> Why us: twenty years inside Irish procurement, encoded into the product — a moat a new entrant can't cross. Traction: our first pilot goes live in August with a Section 39 body, and we're in active conversations across the public sector, including the HSE.
>
> We're raising to convert these pilots into annual contracts and land our first agencies. Real problem, working product, live pilot — and we give public bodies the evidence before the question arrives. Thank you.

**Delivery tip:** Hit the three facts in the first sentence (product, pilot, market). Name the four modules in one breath (router, contracts, budget, audit) — this version wins on pace and platform breadth. Note: the €22bn market figure is spoken only; it's not on a slide, so be ready to source it if asked (OGP data).

## Which to use

- **Default for NDRC pitch day:** **Type B (Governance Shield).** It's the strongest opening for a room you need to grab fast, and the accountability angle is your most defensible wedge. The €5k–€50k invisible-spend line is now built in as the second hook.
- **If the panel skews investor / traction-focused:** **Type C.** Leads with full-stack platform (router + contracts + budget).
- **If you want warmth and the founder story to carry it:** **Type A.**

**Hybrid worth trying:** Type B's cold open (the three names) → Type A's "it's not that people are careless" framing → **estates-manager quote** (fear) → €5k–€50k invisible layer → Type C's full-stack product close → **forty-years endorsement** (validation) → ask.

**Quote pairing:** Use estates-manager mid-pitch (Slide 2–4 territory); use forty-years endorsement on traction/close (Slide 7–8). Fear first, proof last.
