## The principle

You have just personally executed a 327-finding multi-discipline review on a real Stage 2C HSE tender pack. You know what to look for. For the first 3–10 packs VerifIQ ships, **you can be the sole reviewer** — provided you are honest about your scope and disciplined about your throughput.

This is the right shoestring move. It saves the entire panel honorarium line in Doc 15 (€38k–€55k year 1) and removes coordination overhead. It also makes the trust-signal personal and verifiable: customers know who signs.

The trap to navigate: discipline scope. Your initials carry weight in some disciplines and not others. The plan below sets the rules.

## What you can sign as the sole reviewer

Within your domain of competence — Irish public-sector procurement, capital governance, CWMF/PW-CF, compliance, document-set hygiene — you can credibly stamp the audit log on the following finding categories:

| Category | You can sign | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Contract-form coherence (PW-CF variants, LD mechanics, dates) | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| CWMF / OGP procurement risk | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| Tender-pack document hygiene (versions, dates, registers, completeness) | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| BCAR doc-set completeness (not BCAR Assigned Certifier function) | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| Cross-discipline coordination (spec vs schedule vs drawing) | ✅ | Direct expertise — this is the killer skill |
| Cost / QS coordination (BoQ vs spec coordination) | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| Address / title block / metadata anomalies | ✅ | Direct expertise |
| Standards prefix / typo flags (e.g., "I.S. EN 10101" does not exist) | ✅ | Pattern recognition |

For these findings, your reviewer initials go on the audit log. The audit log entry reads something like: *"Reviewed by L. Doolan, founder, VerifIQ (GovIQ Ltd). Capital governance and procurement specialism."*

## What you cannot sign as the sole reviewer

These categories require chartered specialism that you do not hold. Findings still surface — they just cannot carry the "chartered reviewer signed" stamp until a qualified specialist is brought in.

| Category | You cannot sign as chartered reviewer | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Architectural design adequacy / TGD B compliance | RIAI required | Mark "AI-surfaced · pending chartered review · architecture" |
| Structural design / Eurocode application | CEng C&S required | Mark "AI-surfaced · pending chartered review · structures" |
| M&E specification adequacy | CEng MEP required | Mark "AI-surfaced · pending chartered review · M&E" |
| Fire engineering / cause-and-effect | FSE / IFSE required | Mark "AI-surfaced · pending chartered review · fire" |
| BCAR Assigned Certifier function itself | RIAI/EI/SCSI registered AC | Refuse engagement — out of VerifIQ scope |
| Detailed energy / Part L compliance | SEAI-registered EED | Mark "AI-surfaced · pending chartered review · energy" |

For these, three handling options exist — pick one per pack at intake.

### Option A — Refuse the pack until specialist on board

For early packs where the customer's hot-button finding categories sit outside your discipline, politely decline. *"VerifIQ's first cohort is focused on procurement, contract-form, and coordination findings. We're bringing chartered M&E and fire reviewers into the cohort over the next two months — happy to put you on the list for that release."*

Use this when: the customer's pack is dominated by M&E or fire risk, and they explicitly need those signed off.

### Option B — Ship findings clearly marked "AI-surfaced, not chartered-reviewed for this discipline"

The pack is processed and findings are surfaced. The output register has two columns: one for findings within your scope (chartered-reviewed, signed) and one for findings outside it (AI-surfaced, unreviewed). The cover page is unambiguous about which is which.

Use this when: the customer wants the full read and accepts that some categories carry a different trust signal.

### Option C — Per-pack specialist honorarium

You retain a one-off specialist (RIAI architect, CEng M&E, FSE) for that single pack at €150–€350 honorarium. They review only the findings in their discipline. You sign the procurement/contract/coordination findings; they sign theirs. The output looks fully panel-reviewed.

Use this when: the customer is a flagship pilot, the pack is high-stakes, the revenue justifies it (Tier III+).

### Recommended default

Start with **Option B** as the default and migrate packs to **Option C** as your network of specialist contacts grows. **Option A** is the polite refusal for packs you can't credibly read.

## Marketing language — the honesty rule

This is the trap. The website currently implies a panel of chartered reviewers (RIAI · EI · SCSI · ACEI · OGP · HSE marks on `index.html`). If the reviewer is in fact just you, those marks are misleading and create exactly the legal exposure the Global Legal Council exists to prevent.

**Until panel members are signed and operational, every customer surface must say:**

> *"Pilot packs in this cohort are reviewed by Liam Doolan, founder, with [N] years' experience in Irish public-sector capital delivery and procurement. Discipline-specialist reviewers are being brought into the cohort sequentially over the coming weeks — Architecture, M&E, Fire, and Structures. Until each specialist is in place, findings in that discipline are surfaced but marked 'AI-surfaced · pending chartered review.'"*

The website strip currently showing six chartered-body marks (RIAI · EI · SCSI · ACEI · OGP · HSE) must be re-labelled. Replace with:

> *"Standards we read against: RIAI · Engineers Ireland · SCSI · ACEI · OGP · HSE — corpora indexed, not affiliations."*

That single word change ("Standards we read against" not "Reviewed by") covers the honesty gap.

## Throughput cap

You can review **2–4 packs per week** as a sole reviewer while doing everything else founder-related (sales, engineering oversight, customer onboarding, fundraise). Beyond that, quality drops and the SLA breaks.

**Hard cap to publish:** *"VerifIQ's pilot cohort is currently scoped to 4 paid packs per week. New customers join a waitlist for the next available slot."*

This becomes a feature, not a bug. Scarcity is on-brand for the atelier register. It also gates demand at a level you can credibly serve.

**Track:** number of packs in queue + estimated wait per tier. Publish honestly on the pricing page. *"Tier II — Mid: next slot opens [date]."*

## PI / liability — the careful bit

You are reviewing AS founder/director of GovIQ Ltd, with VerifIQ activity covered under GovIQ's Tech E&O insurance (with the VerifIQ rider). You are **not** offering your personal professional opinion under your personal PI.

Two specific rules:

1. **The audit-log signature is your role + entity, not just your name.** Format: *"Reviewed by L. Doolan, Director, VerifIQ (a GovIQ Ltd product) — capital governance specialism."* Not *"Reviewed by L. Doolan, [your chartered title if any]."*
2. **Your personal professional title (whatever charter you hold) is not invoked as endorsement.** You are reviewing in your VerifIQ-founder capacity, not in your personal-charter capacity. Customers must not infer that your personal chartered status backs the finding. This is critical for keeping the locked legal posture intact.

If you do hold a chartered status (SCSI / OGP-list QS / PSDP / etc.), it appears in your bio but NOT next to your reviewer signature on findings.

## Upgrade triggers — when you stop being sole reviewer

Don't be the sole reviewer forever. The triggers below force the upgrade.

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| 4 paid packs/week sustained for 4 weeks | Recruit panel chair as Phase 3 item (Doc 15 line 16) |
| Customer explicitly asks for chartered RIAI / CEng signature in a discipline you don't hold | Bring in a per-pack specialist (Option C) and decide whether to formalise that relationship |
| Pre-seed pitch deck assembled | Panel chair + 2 named specialists must be in place to credibly answer "who reviews?" |
| €25k cumulative VerifIQ revenue | Formalise per-pack specialist relationships into deferred-honorarium standing arrangements |
| First customer escalation / dispute | Bring in a panel chair before the next pack, regardless of any other trigger |

The first three triggers are typical and predictable. The last two are the danger signals — if either fires, the solo phase ends immediately.
